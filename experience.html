Heart Rate Research

Research in Progress

Our current research focuses on studying the impact of heart rate variability in Division II Men's Soccer players at Seton Hill University. This project, which is funded by a grant, is planned to span approximately two years. Collaborating with me on this study are Dr. Brian Larouere and Dr. Jared Burns.

We are evaluating multiple attributes that influence heart health, including baseline measurements such as the 1.5-mile run test for VO2 Max, bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA) for body composition, and the T-test for agility. These assessments are designed to gauge the players' overall fitness components. The primary objective of our research is to analyze the physiological factors that influence heart rate data for each individual player.
